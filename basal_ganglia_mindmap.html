Key Cell Type
Medium Spiny Neurons (MSNs) â€” Overview
  • Found in caudate & putamen
  • Target of incoming cortical axons
  • Large dendritic trees integrate info from cortex, thalamus, brainstem
  • Axons converge on SNpr and GPi
  • Give rise to inhibitory GABAergic projections
  • At rest: hyperpolarized, very little spontaneous activity
  • Need many excitatory inputs to fire
  • Activity may signal decision to move

  • Dopaminergic inputs (SNpc) â†’ contact MSNs at base of dendritic spines, close to cortical inputs â†’ modulate cortical effects
Integration Scale
MSN Convergence
  • Each MSN receives very few inputs from each cortical axon
  • Each cortical axon contacts many MSNs
  • Single MSN integrates from thousands of cortical neurons

D1 Receptors
Dopamine on Direct Pathway
  • Dopamine acting at D1 receptors enhances activity of the direct pathway
  • Promotes movement facilitation
D2 Receptors
Dopamine on Indirect Pathway
  • Dopamine acting at D2 receptors suppresses activity of the indirect pathway

Net Effect
Overall Dopamine Action
  • Because indirect pathway opposes direct pathway:
  • Net effect of dopamine = decrease inhibitory outflow from BG
  • Promotes movement execution
  • Source: Substantia Nigra pars compacta (SNpc)
Healthy vs Diseased
Dopamine in Health
  • Healthy brain: dopamine at D1 & D2 â†’ decrease inhibitory outflow â†’ allows excitation of upper motor neurons
  • Parkinson's: dopamine from SNpc reduced â†’ harder to initiate & terminate movement â†’ more tonic inhibition â†’ decreased excitation

Key Facts
Parkinson's â€” Overview
  • Major degeneration of the nervous system
  • Described in 1817 by Dr. James Parkinson
  • Hypokinetic movement disorder
  • Progressive loss of dopamine in neurons of substantia nigra pars compacta
  • Cause of degeneration not known
  • Substantia nigra degenerates (visible on autopsy)
  • Can be associated with Dementia
  • Also associated with dyskinesias
  • Localized degeneration â†’ rich opportunity for therapeutic intervention

HUNTINGTON'S DISEASE (Hyperkinetic)
Key Facts
Huntington's â€” Overview
  • Neurons from the caudate degenerate
  • Hyperkinetic movement disorder
  • Caudate and putamen shrink (visible on autopsy)
  • Leads to ballistic, choreic movements
Circuit Changes
Huntington's Pathophysiology (Indirect Pathway)
  • Caudate MSNs (D2) degenerate â†’ absence of normal inhibitory input to GPe
  • GPe cells become more active
  • GPe over-inhibits STN â†’ reduces excitatory output of STN to GPi
  • GPi = less inhibitory output
  • VA/VL thalamus = less tonic inhibition
  • Upper motoneurons activated by inappropriate signals
  • Frontal cortex = increased excitation
Hemiballismus (Related)
STN Damage Syndrome
  • Indirect pathway = mechanism to "brake" or "focus" direct pathway
  • STN disorders â†’ remove excitatory input to GPi & reticulata â†’ abnormally reduce inhibitory outflow
  • Hemiballismus: violent, involuntary limb movements from STN damage
  • Involuntary movements from abnormal discharges of upper motoneurons receiving less tonic inhibition
Experimental Evidence
Muscimol Injection (GABA Agonist)
  • Muscimol (GABA agonist) injected into substantia nigra pars reticulata
  • SNpr is inhibitory onto superior colliculus
  • Muscimol â†’ decreases tonic inhibition of superior colliculus
  • Result: monkeys generate spontaneous, irrepressible saccades
  • Resembles involuntary movements of hemiballismus and Huntington's

Introduction
Beyond Motor Control
  • Both Parkinson's & HD associated with cognitive & emotional dysregulation â†’ hints at broader BG roles
  • BG plays key role in cognitive & affective behaviors
  • Parallel loops originate in different regions of cerebral cortex
Body Movement Loop
Motor Loop
  • Cortical input: Primary motor, premotor, supplementary motor cortex
  • Striatum: Putamen
  • Pallidum: Lateral globus pallidus, internal segment
  • Thalamus: Ventral lateral and ventral anterior nuclei
  • Output: Frontal cortex (motor areas)
Prefrontal Loop
Cognitive Loop
  • Cortical input: Dorsolateral prefrontal cortex
  • Striatum: Anterior caudate
  • Pallidum: GPi, substantia nigra pars reticulata
  • Thalamus: Mediodorsal and ventral anterior nuclei
  • Output: Dorsolateral prefrontal cortex
  • Function: May regulate initiation & termination of cognitive processes
Limbic Loop
Emotional & Motivational Loop
  • Cortical input: Anterior cingulate, orbitofrontal cortex
  • Also receives from: Amygdala, hippocampus, orbitofrontal, anterior cingulate, temporal cortex
  • Striatum: Ventral striatum (nucleus accumbens)
  • Pallidum: Ventral pallidum
  • Thalamus: Mediodorsal nucleus
  • Output: Cortical targets
  • Function: May regulate emotional & motivated behaviour & transitions between mood states
  • Ventral striatum particularly implicated in reward, motivation and emotion
